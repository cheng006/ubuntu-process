UBUNTU

HISTORY

The Ubuntu typeface, part of the Ubuntu project, was developed by London-based type foundry Dalton Maag, with funding by Canonical Ltd, to serve as the default font for the Ubuntu Linux operating system. Designed to be free and open-source, it prioritized readability on screens and in print, accommodating various languages and character sets. The font was under development for nearly nine months, with only a limited initial release through a beta program, until September 2010.

This modern, clean, humanist-style, and legible OpenType-based font family included regular, bold, italic, and bold italic variants. It aligned with Ubuntu's open-source ethos, releasing under the Ubuntu Font License.

DALTON

MAAG

Dalton Maag is a typeface design studio, founded in 1991. They work with clients and agencies worldwide to enhance their typographic expression, from logo refinement and licensing to custom font suites.

MORE THAN JUST

A TYPEFACE

LINUX

Ubuntu Linux, initiated by Canonical Ltd. in 2004, is a popular open-source operating system. It's known for its user-friendliness, regular releases, and extensive community support. Ubuntu uses the GNOME desktop environment and is based on Debian. It rapidly gained a large user base due to its ease of use.

AFRICAN

PROVERB

"Ubuntu" is an African
philosophy that emphasizes the interconnectedness of humanity. It means "I am because we are" or "humanity towards others." It underscores the idea that our well-being and identity are linked to the well-being and humanity of others. It promotes compassion, community, and a sense of shared responsibility, encouraging people to treat one another with respect, empathy, and kindness.
